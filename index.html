Texture Lab
is a space for making, feeling, and experimenting.
From hand-made textures to digital patterns, this is where I share my process—sometimes with tutorials, sometimes just the mess and magic of making.


Want to see what happens with clay? Or what a scanner can do? Tap the images or texts to filter the archive by technique and get inspired.

Analogue Experiments

A collection of textures and marks created through direct, hands-on processes. Using brushes, gel plates, brayers, and even my own hands, I explored the possibilities of paint, layering, and pressure. These experiments capture the tactile and unpredictable beauty of analogue making — the smudges, smears, and surprises that digital tools can’t replicate.

Let's start!

Digital Experiments

A series of compositions made using scanners, cameras, and digital software. These works explore how light, layering, and timing affect the final image. From scanner distortion to photographic textures and post-production enhancements, each piece captures a moment where the digital process becomes a tool for unexpected beauty and controlled chance.

Let's start!

Archive

A collection of outcomes created through both analogue and digital processes. This space holds the results of hands-on exploration — textures, compositions, and visual experiments that have taken shape through making. Each piece is a snapshot of where intuition met technique.
